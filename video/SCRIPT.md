# Meridian — 3-minute demo video script

~400 words ≈ 3:00 at ~130 wpm. Beats are timed to the app; captions recommended (many judges
watch muted). Record at 1080p/60fps. Auto-demo link for a clean take: `/console?run=hormuz&i=70`.

---

### 0:00–0:15 · Hook — the landing globe (`/`)
> "India runs the world's fifth-largest economy on oil it doesn't control. Nearly nine of every ten
> barrels are imported — and much of it sails through **one strait**." *(globe spinning; red = Hormuz corridors)*

### 0:15–0:35 · The stakes — scroll to the stats
> "Eighty-eight percent imported. Forty-two percent through the Strait of Hormuz. Nine and a half
> days of reserve. When geopolitics tests that — and it does, every few years — prices spike before
> anyone can respond." *(stats count up; scroll into the 2025 Brent-spike scene drawing itself)*

### 0:35–0:50 · The gap
> "In June 2025 a naval standoff sent Brent up eight percent in a session. Every sensor saw it —
> the AIS feeds, the price screens, the news. **None of them acted.** That's the gap Meridian closes."

### 0:50–1:05 · Enter the console
> "This is the console." *(click Enter the console)* "A live digital twin of India's energy supply —
> real tankers, real corridors, real refineries. Watch what happens when I trigger a Hormuz closure."

### 1:05–1:45 · The directed run (the core — let it breathe)
> "Meridian takes the wheel." *(Simulate; camera flies to Hormuz, crisis ripple)* "It reads the
> signals — geopolitical, maritime, market, sanctions — and fuses them. Then it models the **cascade**:
> crude price, refinery run-rates, pump price, GDP — every number auditable, not a black box."
> *(cascade animates; risk index climbs; Brent jumps)*

### 1:45–2:15 · Act + verify (the moat)
> "Now it acts — ranking alternative crude corridors — and **verifies each one** before a human sees
> it. The cheapest barrels, Russian Urals, are **auto-rejected on a price-cap breach**. The
> Fujairah Hormuz-bypass is promoted to number one, all checks green." *(reroute arcs draw; cards spring in)*
> "Signal to a verified, executable plan — in seconds." *(point at the timer chip)*

### 2:15–2:35 · Close the loop
> "And I can execute it." *(click Approve & route)* "Cargo committed, charter issued, national
> exposure eases. Incumbents stop at the signal. Meridian closes the loop."

### 2:35–3:00 · Impact & ask
> "A control tower for national energy security — refiners, government, ports — generalising to any
> critical import: LNG, fertiliser, minerals. It runs free, works live, and it's built. Meridian."
> *(clean brand frame / URL)*
